## Color Usage Hierarchy

Based on the brand color usage distribution — the pie chart shows how often each color should appear in brand work:

### Primary (dominant)
- **Purple (#7671F9)** — the largest presence in the brand color system
  - Use most frequently across all brand surfaces
  - Primary brand color for UI elements, accents, and brand moments

### Secondary (strong supporting)
- **Orange-Yellow (#FFA500)** — second-highest usage
  - Major supporting color, pairs with Purple
  - Warm counterpart to the cool primary

## Secondary Colors — Data Visuals & Diagrams

### Data Visual Backgrounds
- **#F9F9FF** — primary plain background for data visuals, dashboards, charts
  - Use when gradient backgrounds are too busy for dense data
  - Neutral, clean, recedes behind content
- **#f2f2f7** — extra background grey
  - Secondary background option when layering or distinguishing zones
  - Cards, panels, alternating rows
- **#FFFAEE** — warm background for diagrams
  - Can also be used as background fill for diagrams and flowcharts
  - Warmer alternative to the cool #F9F9FF

### Data Visual Text
- **#00000** — primary text color for data visuals
  - Pure black for maximum readability on light backgrounds
  - Body copy, labels, axis text, legend text
- **#E9E9ED** — grey text
  - Use for secondary/supporting text in data visuals
  - Axis labels, annotations, helper text that should recede

### Usage
- 🔒 Use these ONLY for data-heavy surfaces (dashboards, charts, tables, analytics)
- 🔒 Never use on brand/marketing surfaces — gradient backgrounds + white/black are the brand defaults
- 🔒 #F9F9FF and #f2f2f7 provide clean neutral backdrops that let data be foreground
- 🧪 Status: Decided 2026-06-26 / Mahnoor

## Gradient System

> All gradients share one non-negotiable property: **a halftone pixel/square grid texture baked in**.
> The grid is denser (larger squares) in high-saturation areas and fades to invisible in white zones.
> This is what makes the gradient feel printed/physical rather than digital. Never use without it.

### Variant 1 — Soft / Airy (Final 1)
- Character: White-dominant. Color appears as soft ambient washes at the edges — feels like light through a prism
- Dominant tones: Near-white / very light sky blue (left), pale blush pink (center), warm amber-orange (top-right corner)
- Energy level: Lowest — most delicate. Good for backgrounds where text needs to be legible on top
- Halftone texture: Present but subtle — dots are small, hard to see in white zones, more visible in colored corners
- Use it for: Presentation slide backgrounds, hero sections where content sits on top, light-touch brand moments
- Status: 🔒 · Source: Final 1.png

### Variant 2 — Full Saturation / Warm Dominant (Final 2)
- Character: High energy, warm. Amber-orange fills the left and lower half; pink-lavender sweeps upper-right; sky blue bleeds in from the right edge with a bright white hotspot center-right
- Dominant tones: Amber-gold (#F5B565 range), peachy pink-lavender (upper-right), sky blue (right edge), white hotspot
- Energy level: Highest — most vivid. The "signature" gradient
- Halftone texture: Clearly visible throughout — square grid prominent in all colored zones
- Use it for: Gradient panels (right side of split layout), covers, backgrounds where the gradient IS the hero. Gumloop wordmark sits white on this
- Status: 🔒 · Source: Final 2.png

### Variant 3 — Pink / Warm Blush (Crop 4)
- Character: Soft pink-salmon dominant. Warm, gentle. Upper half fades to near-white; bottom-right glows amber-orange; lower-left has a lavender tint
- Dominant tones: Blush pink (#F8D9E2 range), peachy salmon (#F6C4CD range), warm orange-amber (bottom-right), soft lavender (lower-left)
- Energy level: Medium — warmer and softer than Variant 2, more saturated than Variant 1
- Halftone texture: Clearly visible — especially in the pink and orange zones
- Use it for: Warmer/softer brand contexts, announcement cards, diploma panels, any surface that needs warmth over energy
- Status: 🔒 · Source: Crop 4.png

### Variant 4 — Cool / Blue-Lavender (Crop 5)
- Character: Cool, calm, airy. Sky blue and periwinkle dominate; warm blush appears as a subtle glow in the bottom-right corner
- Dominant tones: Sky blue (#C8DCF1 range), periwinkle-lavender (#BCBEE4 range), pale blue (top-left), warm blush accent (bottom-right)
- Energy level: Low-medium — the "cool" counterpart to Variant 2
- Halftone texture: Clearly visible throughout — square grid prominent in the blue and lavender zones
- Use it for: Cool-toned brand moments, pairing with Variant 2 on opposite panels, contexts where blue-family colors need to lead
- Status: 🔒 · Source: Crop 5.png

## Gradient Usage Rules

- 🔒 Halftone pixel/square grid texture is **mandatory** on all gradient surfaces — no exceptions
- 🔒 The texture is denser (more visible) in high-saturation zones and fades toward white — this creates depth
- 🔒 All 4 variants pull from the same warm-to-cool color family — they are tonal shifts, not different systems
- 🧪 Variant 2 is the "signature" — use when the gradient needs to make a statement
- 🧪 Variant 1 is the background workhorse — text stays legible on top
- 🧪 Crop into the gradient to find the mood you need — the system is designed to be cropped
- 🔒 White hotspots are intentional — the gradient bleeds to white, it doesn't have a hard edge
